Student Regulation: Tardiness during Onsite Learning

Please follow the student regulations! Try not to get tardiness during onsite learning, but if you do, follow these regulations:

This regulation is about Tardiness during Onsite learning.

Tardiness During Learing

  • Students must report to the teacher on duty to complete an admission slip before entering class
  • Students are strictly limited to a maximum of three late arrivals
  • On the fourth instance of tardiness, the homeroom teacher issues a verbal warning
  • On the fifth instance, parents receive a written reminder by text message from the Vice-Principal for Student Affairs, and the student mustsubmit a written pledge not to be late again.
  • On the sixth instance, the student receives Warning Letter I (SP I) and an “Satisfactory (S)” grade in the General Behavior – Discipline section of the  report slip

Absence Due to illness - Onsite and Remote Learning

  1. Parents must notify the homeroomteacher on the same day,sending a photo of the doctor’s note via WhatsApp, if the student is absent due to illness (onsite or remote)
  2. For onsite learning, a medical certificate must be submitted within three days of returning to school if the illness lasts more than one day.
  3. Failure to submit the certificate, or absence without formal parental notice during onsite/remote learning,is recorded as unexplained absence (alpha)
  4. A 5 % deduction is applied to the subject grade on the day the total unexplained absences reach five.

    Leaving class for external matters – Onsite learning

    • Students must use a hall pass to visit the restroom or refill water bottles
    • They then obtain an off-campusformfromthe Vice-Principal for Student Affairs with the parents’ letter attached
    • The completed formis given to the security officer before leaving, and the parent/guardian signs it on collection
    • Security staff will ask the student’s pick-up person to sign the off-campus  form

    Leaving class due to illness – onsite learning

    1. A seriously ill studentmay go home early after parental confirmation of the authorized pick-up person.
    2. The student obtains an off-campusformfromthe Vice-Principal for Student Affairs and submits it to security for signature.
    3. The completed formis given to the security officer before leaving, and the parent/guardian signs it on collection.
    4. Security staff will ask the student’s pick-up person to sign the off-campus  form

    Leaving class without permission – onsite learning

    • Student who leaves class without permission receives a verbal warning and the incident is recorded.
    • After four such incidents,the student must submit a written statement.
    • Students will receive a First Warning Letter (SP I) if a similar violation occurs in the future).

    Catch-up assessments – Onsite Learning

    1. Students have the right to take a make-up test with fullscore if they are absent due to:
      1. illness(proven by a doctor’s note),
      2. bereavement due to a death in the family, or
      3. participation in a competition assigned by the school.
      For points(a) and (b), students must submit a permission letter to their homeroom teacher no later than 3 days after returning to school.
    2. Students may request to take a make-up test if they are participating in an exam or competition on a personal basis, by submitting an official participation letter no later than 1 week in advance.
    3. Requests to take a make-up test due to other reasons are subject to the principal’s discretion. The maximumscore for themake-up test will be the passing grade (KKM) if the request does not meetthe conditions stated in points one and two, and is not considered an urgent matter.
    4. For both onsite and distance learning, students will take the make-up test after all learning activities are completed (after school), according to the schedule set by the subject teacher.
    5. Students who fail to attend the make-up test atthe scheduled time will not be given a rescheduled session.
    6. School does not provide shuttle services for make-up tests.

    Remedial Assessments

    • Students who have not met the Minimum Passing Criteria (KKM) have the right to take a remedial session, scheduled (afterschool/after the learning process is completed) by the subject teacher.
    • Students who are unable to attend the remedial session due to certain reasons may take a remedial make-up, following the same provisions as the make-up test policy.
    • Students who fail to attend the remedial session at the scheduled time will not be given a rescheduled session.
    • The maximum remedial score equals the passing grade.
    • The school does not provide shuttle services for remedial tests.

    Use of mobile phones / electronic devices – Onsite Learning

    1. Students are allowed to bring mobile phones to school; however, they are not permitted to turn them on or use them during school hours, except with the subject teacher’s approval to support the learning topic.
    2. Students are required to turn off and submit their mobile phones to their respective homeroom teachers before starting the morning devotion.
    3. Students must submit their mobile phones to their Homeroom (HR) teacher's designated box during devotion.
    4. If a phone is needed for a lesson, students may retrieve it from their HR teacher but must return it to the box immediately after the class.
    5. Phones are not permitted to be kept in bags or anywhere else in the classroom.
    6. Students who misuse the policy on mobile phone or other electronic device usage will receive a consequence from the school in the form of a red violation slip.
    7. If a violation occurs, the student will receive a red violation slip and a consequence in the form of their mobile phone or other electronic device being confiscated by the teacher and returned to the parents after 3 days by the Vice Principal for Student Affairs.
    8. If a similar violation occurs in the future, the student will receive a red violation slip and a consequence in the form of their mobile phone or other electronic device being confiscated by the teacher and returned to the parents maximally on report card distribution day by the Vice Principal for Student Affairs.

    Delivery of items to students – onsite learning

    • Students are only allowed to contact their parents or guardians to request the delivery of lunch and/or medication.
    • Lunch and/or medication sentfrom home must be labeled with the student’s full name and class.
    • Students must collect delivered items from the designated rack in the  canteen.

    Classlockers Usage

    • Each student is assigned one locker outside the home-base classroom.
    • Each student who wishesto use a locker isrequired to submit a deposit of Rp. 50,000 (fifty thousand rupiah) to their respective homeroom teacher.
    • The deposit is refunded at year-end if the key is returned in good condition. However, students who fail to return the locker key at the end of the academic year in good condition or have lost it will not have their deposit money refunded.
    • During the academic year, if a student needs to duplicate their locker key, the homeroom teacher will lend the master key. The cost of duplication will be borne by the student.
